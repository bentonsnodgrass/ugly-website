What is Mustard?

Mustard is what you make it!

Mustard is a condiment made from the seeds of a mustard plant. The seeds are ground and mixed with water, vinegar, lemon juice, wine, or other liquids, salt, and often other flavorings and spices, to create a paste or sauce ranging in color from bright yellow to dark brown. Mustard is typically used as a condiment on sandwiches, hot dogs, hamburgers, and other foods. It can also be used as an ingredient in sauces, dressings, and marinades. Mustard is a versatile condiment that can be enjoyed on a variety of foods. It is a good source of vitamins and minerals, including vitamin C, potassium, and iron. Mustard is also a low-calorie condiment, making it a healthy choice for people who are watching their weight.

Types of Mustards

  • Dijon mustard: A strong, flavorful mustard made from brown mustard seeds.
  • Yellow mustard: A mild, sweet mustard made from white mustard seeds.
  • Honey mustard: A mustard made with honey, which gives it a sweet and tangy flavor.
  • Stone-ground mustard: A mustard made with whole mustard seeds, which gives it a coarse texture and a strong flavor.
  • Creole mustard: A mustard made with a variety of spices, including cayenne pepper, which gives it a spicy flavor.

Basic Mustard

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup mustard seeds
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup white wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Grind the mustard seeds in a spice grinder or mortar and pestle until they are a coarse powder.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine the ground mustard seeds, water, vinegar, salt, sugar, turmeric, ginger, and pepper.
  3. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat, stirring constantly.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Remove the pan from the heat and let the mustard cool completely.
  6. Store the mustard in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.
